Garlic (Allium sativum)

Garlic (Allium sativum) is a bulbous plant widely cultivated for its pungent cloves used as seasoning. It prefers well-drained soil and a cool growing season.

  • Water: Keep soil moist but avoid waterlogging.
  • Light: Full sun for best growth and flavor.
  • Soil: Loose, well-drained fertile soil rich in organic matter.
  • Temperature: Prefers a cool period followed by warm weather.
  • Growth: Forms bulbs underground; requires regular care and pest management.

Tips: Mulch to conserve moisture and suppress weeds. Rotate crops to prevent disease.

Common Problems: Watch for onion maggot, white rot, and fungal infections.
