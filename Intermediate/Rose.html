Rose (Rosa spp.)

Roses are classic flowering shrubs prized for their fragrant and colorful blossoms. They require well-drained soil, regular watering, and good air circulation.

  • Water: Deep watering 1-2 times per week, especially during flowering.
  • Light: Full sun for at least 6 hours.
  • Soil: Fertile, well-drained soil rich in organic matter.
  • Temperature: Prefer temperate climates; protect from extreme heat or cold.
  • Growth: Prune regularly to maintain shape and promote blooms.

Tips: Use mulch to retain moisture and control weeds. Fertilize in spring and after flowering.

Common Problems: Watch for aphids, black spot, and powdery mildew.
