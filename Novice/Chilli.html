Chili (Capsicum annuum)

Chili peppers are popular spicy fruits known for their heat and flavor. They grow best in warm climates with plenty of sunlight.

  • Water: Regular watering to keep soil moist but not waterlogged.
  • Light: Full sun, at least 6 hours daily.
  • Soil: Well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.
  • Temperature: Warm temperatures, ideally between 70-85°F (21-29°C).
  • Growth: Requires staking or support as the plant matures.

Tips: Use mulch and avoid overwatering. Harvest when fruits fully ripen for best flavor.

Common Problems: Watch for aphids, whiteflies, and fungal infections.
