Drumstick (Moringa oleifera)

Drumstick tree is a fast-growing, drought-resistant plant used for its nutritious seed pods and leaves. It is commonly grown in tropical and subtropical regions.

  • Water: Moderate watering; allow soil to dry between watering.
  • Light: Full sun.
  • Soil: Well-drained, sandy or loamy soil.
  • Temperature: Prefers warm climates, sensitive to frost.
  • Growth: Can grow up to 10 meters tall; prune regularly for manageable size.

Tips: Use organic fertilizer and mulch to enhance growth. Harvest pods when young and tender.

Common Problems: Be mindful of pests like aphids and caterpillars; root rot may occur in poorly drained soils.
