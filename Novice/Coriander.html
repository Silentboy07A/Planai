Coriander (Coriandrum sativum)

Coriander, also known as cilantro, is a versatile herb grown worldwide for its flavorful leaves and seeds. It prefers cool weather and well-drained soil.

  • Water: Water regularly, keeping soil moist but not soggy.
  • Light: Full sun to partial shade.
  • Soil: Fertile, well-draining soil rich in organic matter.
  • Temperature: Thrives in mild to cool conditions.
  • Growth: Fast-growing leaves, often harvested before flowering.

Tips: Succession planting ensures fresh leaves all season. Harvest leaves early for best flavor.

Common Problems: Watch out for bolting in hot weather and pests like aphids.
