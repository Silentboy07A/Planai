Aloe Vera (Aloe barbadensis miller)

Aloe Vera is a succulent plant known for its thick, fleshy leaves filled with gel used for medicinal and skincare purposes. It is drought-tolerant and thrives with minimal care.

  • Water: Water sparingly; allow soil to dry completely between watering.
  • Light: Bright, indirect sunlight to partial shade.
  • Soil: Well-drained, sandy soil.
  • Temperature: Prefers warm climates, sensitive to freezing temperatures.
  • Growth: Slow to moderate growth; can be propagated from offsets.

Tips: Avoid overwatering; aloe thrives better in slightly dry conditions. Use pots with drainage holes.

Common Problems: Root rot caused by overwatering, spider mites, and fungal infections.
